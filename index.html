ParkourFormer

Integrating Predictive Supervision and Sequence Modeling into Parkour Locomotion

Yanheng Mai1,2,†, Wenhao Xu2,3,†, Zirui Huang2,3, Yifei Fu2,3, Shengwei Dong2,3,
Xinjue Wang3, Kailun Huang2,3, Renjing Xu1,*

1The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (Guangzhou)   2CLAI-LAB  
3South China Agricultural University 4Guangdong University of Technology

Abstract

Whole-body control(WBC) for legged robots has a traditional process paradigm, mapping a single current observation to an action using only past information. In order to the agile control, We introduce a new locomotion training paradigm that reframes locomotion as a sequence-to-sequence (Seq2Seq) task, endowing the policy with rich implicit supervisory signals through explicit future state prediction and enabling a conditional decision process that jointly reasons over past context and anticipated future states.We present ParkourFormer, a Transformer-based framework, processing a stack of the past 8 observation frames by position encoder. The current state acts as a query in a multi-head cross-attention mechanism over historical keys and values. A lightweight future observation prediction head forecasts the robot's proprioceptive state for the next two timesteps; this head is directly supervised by future ground-truth observations obtained from policy rollouts. The predicted future embeddings are concatenated with the historical features to jointly produce the current action delta. Experiments in simulation and on a physical humanoid demonstrate that ParkourFormer establishes a new state-of-the-art, outperforming the strongest baselines by 30.49% in success rate of multi-terrian parkour. ParkourFormer achieves uniform high performance across all training terrains with a single policy, revealing that sequence-level modeling with future-state prediction enables balanced multi-terrain optimization and superior generalization for whole-body locomotion control.
